Autumn Recipes

As the nights draw in and the long summer holidays end, there is a back-to-school feel in the air. Autumn with its golden mellow light, glorious colours and the rustle of fallen leaves is probably the most beautiful season. Take advantage of the trees being ladened with ripe apples, pears, plums and the hedgerows full of blackberries. Spend this time of year cooking our recipes in the warmth of your kitchen, and enjoy the gentle spices, and toasted nuts.

Why not welcome autumn with one of these favourites?

White Chocolate and Blackberry Fingers.

Perfect for an autumnal afternoon tea or supper. If you don't have a blackberry bush to hand, then of course you can buy cultivated ones. Raspberries and blueberries could always be used instead, if you prefer.

Toad in the Hole.

What better for Hallowe'en than to put a toad on somebody's plate? The batter is flavoured with rosemary, but you could also try a few pinches of paprika or replace the mustard powder with a tablespoon of whole grain mustard.

View Recipe
Parkin

A hearty, comforting ginger cake made with oatmeal and black treacle, parkin originated in the north of England and is customarily eaten on Bonfire Night. It gets better and stickier with time, so once cooled, store in an airtight tin.

View Recipe
Pumkin Soup.

Served in its own raw or baked 'carcas', this soup is a favourite for this time of the year. Witches' fingers cheese straws are great to dip into the soup.
